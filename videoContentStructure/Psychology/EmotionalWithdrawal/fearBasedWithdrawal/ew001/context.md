# Context

## Territory
The Quiet Marriage

## Theme
When intimacy becomes a performance, the heart learns to hide.

## Psychological Truth
The fear of rejection transforms affection into anxiety, turning love's natural rhythm into a carefully managed ritual.

## Characters
**Ethan Morrison (32)**  
Marriage counselor  
Cautious, observant, emotionally reserved  
He watches the world but rarely enters it fully  

**Claire Morrison (30)**  
Graphic designer  
Creative, expressive, deeply intuitive  
She feels what others cannot name  

## Relationship
They've been married for two years, though it feels like three. Their intimacy has shifted from spontaneous connection to calculated moments.

Their shared context includes the early days of their relationship when physical affection was constant and unthinking, followed by the gradual accumulation of expectations that now weigh on their quiet moments together.

## Setting
A modest suburban home in Portland, Oregon. They live in a converted Victorian house with high ceilings and narrow hallways that echo with silence.

The time period is contemporary, marked by the quiet hum of modern life—email notifications, streaming services, and the constant presence of screens that separate them even when they're physically close.

## Emotional Atmosphere
There is a careful stillness that permeates every moment, as if they're walking on eggshells of their own making. The pace of life moves slowly, with long stretches of waiting and watching.

## Visual Language
* Empty spaces in the bedroom where physical contact once lived  
* Ethan's hands hovering near Claire’s shoulder before pulling back  
* The bathroom door left slightly ajar during evening routines  
* Long silences broken only by the sound of turning pages or distant television  
* Ethan's eyes in the mirror during morning routines, reflecting back at himself  

## Ending Emotion
A deep recognition of how fear can make us strangers to the love we thought we knew.
