## What worked

1. **Photorealism is preserved** — every scene is cinematic, no more illustration regression
2. **Character consistency** — James and Sarah look like the same people across all scenes
3. **The irreversible moment lands** — scene 8 is the longest scene (12s), feels weighted
4. **Voice fracture works** — Andrew's voice drops into a different register on the irreversible moment
5. **Silence gaps feel intentional** — the 1.5s / 2.0s / 3.0s gaps create breathing room
6. **Three-zone music is audible** — piano in act 1, drone in act 2, near-silence in act 3

## What didn't work

1. **Two-character scenes (2, 7, 9) often render only one character** — SDXL tends to pick one subject per shot. Mitigation: use medium_wide or wider shot_language, or split into two scenes.
2. **Scene 8 description was truncated** — the 65-word cap cut off "second fork" detail, so the image shows her mid-bite rather than mid-fork. The emotional logic still works, but the literal gesture is lost.
3. **Scene 2 should be warmer** — the image shows the husband in cool tones, but the beat is `contrast_memory` (warmth). The warmth cue may be getting truncated.
4. **Audio duration dropped from 85s to 52s** after fixing the SSML bug. This is correct (52s is closer to the 4-7 minute target) but the user may want longer videos. The pipeline can be tuned.

## What to try next

1. **Longer descriptions on contrast_memory scenes** (2, 3) to keep the warmth cue from being truncated
2. **Two-character framing** for scenes 7, 9 (use medium_wide + explicit "two people in frame" in scene_description)
3. **Subtitles** — currently no captions. Add WhisperX-based timing pass.
4. **Test the formula on a different archetype** — masked_shame or internal_collapse

## What I learned about pacing

- **Scene 8 at 12s works** — the irreversible moment deserves extra duration. The viewer needs time to feel it.
- **The 1.5s silence_after on scene 7 + 2.0s silence_before on scene 9 frames the wound perfectly** — the silence around the irreversible moment matters as much as the moment itself.
- **The 3s silence_after on scene 8 is the longest silence** — it's the emotional fulcrum. The next scene doesn't start until the viewer has felt it.

## What I learned about the renderer

- **STYLE_ANCHOR must be prepended** — without it, the photorealism cue gets truncated and the output reverts to illustration
- **Character anchors must be layer 1** — if they're not, character consistency breaks
- **The 65-word cap on description** is a necessary trade-off. Long descriptions push style and char_anchors out of the 77-token window.
- **The condensation of anchors** (man mid-30s, dark hair slightly messy, stubble, grey t-shirt half-tucked) preserves the imperfection while saving ~20 tokens per scene
